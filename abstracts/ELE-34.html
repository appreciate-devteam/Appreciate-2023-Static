Development of Hydropower Turbine using Polyvinyl Chloride Material in Downspouts

ACERO, Edward L., FALLARCUNA, Justine P., ANDAYA, Elgie S., BACOLOD, Andrea Jasmine R., PAMONAG, Ranch P., TANTUAN, Mel Lovely G., VERANO, Harold Philip N.

The increase in population and energy demand in recent years has resulted in the utilization of renewable energy sources to alleviate the harmful effects of non-renewable energy sources on the environment. Moreover, an untapped hydropower source potential is rainwater from downspouts which previous studies show were implemented as small-scale hydropower systems. However, research about the operation and maintenance of the material found that the problems of small hydropower turbines are majorly caused by failure due to cavitation and silt erosion. As such, the researchers of this study aimed to develop and analyze a pico-hydropower system with dual turbine using polyvinyl chloride as turbine material in downspouts to determine the effectiveness of the material and the pico-hydropower system as a renewable energy source for low-voltage devices. The results show that a 6-blade polyvinyl chloride curved-blade turbine is best in terms of velocity and pressure as compared to an 8-blade and 10-blade curved-blade turbine according to Flow Simulation with Computational Fluid Dynamics. The preliminary testing of the pico-hydropower system with three trials at a 12-feet head downspout loaded with 10-12 liters of water at 24-30 seconds duration produced a maximum power output of 0.46765974 W for a single turbine with load and 0.309814 W for a dual turbine with load.
