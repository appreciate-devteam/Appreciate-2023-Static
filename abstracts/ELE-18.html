Solar Powered IoT-Based Aquaculture Management System with Automated Water Corrective System

MERIN, Jovencio V., DELA CRUZ, Carl Ivan M., BUENAVENTURA, Ace R., CETRON, Louise Carlo G., MARQUEZ, Neil Dominic V., SINGH, Raymond Ian Q.

Aquaculture has a long history in the Philippines, encompassing a wide range of species and growing methods in a variety of settings. Aquaculture is growing far faster than catch fisheries. However, the Philippines' global standing in aquaculture output has been steadily deteriorating. Future Philippine aquaculture development may not be viable. This study aims to investigate the feasibility and effectiveness of using a SMART and sustainable solar-powered IoT system for aquaculture management. This paper presents a solar powered water quality monitoring system with automated water corrective system and filtering system to monitor and maintain the essential water quality parameters for growth of shrimp, such as temperature, pH level, humidity, turbidity, electrical conductivity (EC), total dissolve solids (TDS), total suspended solids (TSS), dissolve oxygen and dissolve carbon dioxide. The collected data is transmitted to a cloud-based platform where it is analyzed and used to adjust the operation of the shrimp farm in real-time. The system also uses aquarium heater, motor for sodium bicarbonate distribution, solenoid valve, water pump, mechanical filtrations and biofiltration as correcting devices to keep the parameters at the desired level, which is best for shrimp growth. Two intensive aquaculture installations, a controlled setup, and a traditional setup, were observed by the proponents to gauge the system's effectiveness and dependability. According to the data collected, the controlled arrangement significantly outperformed the conventional design's yield quality, boosted efficiency, and decreased the work required of shrimp farmers.
